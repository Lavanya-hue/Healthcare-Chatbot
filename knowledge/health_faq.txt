Q: What are the common symptoms of diabetes?
A: Increased thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision, and slow wound healing.

Q: What is hypertension?
A: Hypertension is high blood pressure, a condition where the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high.

Q: What are the symptoms of hypertension?
A: Often no symptoms; severe hypertension may cause headaches, shortness of breath, or nosebleeds.

Q: What is anemia?
A: A condition in which the blood doesn't have enough healthy red blood cells.

Q: What are the symptoms of anemia?
A: Fatigue, pale skin, dizziness, shortness of breath, and cold hands/feet.

Q: What is asthma?
A: A chronic condition that affects the airways, causing difficulty in breathing.

Q: What are common asthma symptoms?
A: Wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath.

Q: What is COVID-19?
A: A viral illness caused by the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2.

Q: What are symptoms of COVID-19?
A: Fever, cough, tiredness, loss of taste/smell, sore throat.

Q: What is dehydration?
A: A condition caused by not having enough fluids in the body.

Q: What are dehydration symptoms?
A: Dry mouth, dizziness, dark urine, fatigue, headache.

Q: What is migraine?
A: A neurological condition causing severe headaches often with nausea and sensitivity to light.

Q: What are migraine symptoms?
A: Pulsating headache, visual disturbances, vomiting, sensitivity to light/sound.

Q: What is obesity?
A: A medical condition involving excessive body fat that increases health risks.

Q: What are the causes of obesity?
A: Poor diet, lack of physical activity, genetics, environment, and certain medical conditions.

Q: What is flu?
A: A contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses.

Q: What are the symptoms of flu?
A: Fever, chills, cough, sore throat, runny nose, muscle aches.

Q: What is a cold?
A: A mild viral infection of the nose and throat.

Q: What are the symptoms of common cold?
A: Sneezing, runny nose, sore throat, cough, mild fever.

Q: What is dengue?
A: A viral infection spread by Aedes mosquitoes, common in tropical regions.

Q: What are the symptoms of dengue?
A: Sudden fever, severe headache, muscle and joint pain, rash, nausea, vomiting. Symptoms typically appear 3-14 days after infection.

Q: How is dengue treated?
A: Rest, fluids, and paracetamol for fever and pain. Avoid aspirin. Seek medical attention if symptoms worsen or bleeding occurs.

Q: Can I take paracetamol for fever?
A: Yes, paracetamol (acetaminophen) is safe for fever. Use as directed on the package. Avoid exceeding 4000mg per day.

Q: What should I do for fever and headache?
A: Rest in a cool environment, drink plenty of fluids, take paracetamol as directed, and monitor your temperature. Seek medical help if fever persists beyond 3 days.

Q: What are the symptoms of diabetes complications?
A: Chest pain, shortness of breath, severe fatigue, blurred vision, numbness in extremities, and kidney problems.

Q: When should I see a doctor for chest pain?
A: Seek immediate medical attention if experiencing chest pain, especially if accompanied by shortness of breath, nausea, or severe symptoms. Call emergency services if severe.
